descriptive statistics #

Descriptive statistics are exactly what you think. They describe what the data is or shows. They do not make a judgement about the data. They do not give any larger inference to a population from a sample. They describe a tendency of the data, or a breadth of the data.

Let's start with a simple dataset of 15 random numbers between 0 and 50.

central tendency

The mean, median, and mode are measures of central tendency. Each are used to show identifying characteristics of a dataset.

mean

A mean is commonly referred to as an average. It is the sum of the points divided by the number of points.

median

A median is the middle point of a dataset. Half the points in the set lie before the median and half the points in the set lie after the median.

mode

The mode is the most commonly occurring point in a dataset.

spread

The range, variance and standard deviation are all measures of spread for a dataset. These measures give an approximation of the breadth of the data.

range

The range is the maximum point in a dataset minus the minimum point in a dataset. In other terms, what does the dataset span?

variance

Variance is the average of the squared differences from the mean. In other words, the standard deviation squared.

standard deviation

The standard deviation is a measure of the amount of variation in a set of data. More commonly thought of as the average of the difference from the mean.

box and whisker plot

A box and whisker plot is a representation of a dataset showing multiple characteristics of that dataset. The plot below is a representation of our dataset.

Starting from the bottom line, called a whisker, we see the lowest possible value of the dataset displayed on the right.

Moving upwards we encounter the "box", with the median value displayed on the right, and the 25th and 75th quartile values displayed on the left. As you might infer from the last sentence, each line in the plot divides a quartile. From the bottom whisker to the bottom line of the box representing 25% of the data values.

sampling #

Sampling is the process of selecting a subset of individuals from a population. The basic theory of sampling is that a subset which has followed correct sampling techniques will be representative of the population. Creating a representative sampling frame would mean that the sample's makeup would closely match that of the population.

The first thing you'll notice when playing with the sample size is just how much your representation of each color can vary from sample to sample. Intuitively, the larger your sample size the more likely the sample is to be a closer approximation to the population and thus representative.
